Teaching Resourcing Affirming Inspiring Networking

This page last updated: 2005/01/28
 

Pictures from the TRAIN 2004 Conference

Here we present a series of photographs that depict an attendee's experience at the TRAIN 2004 Conference – the things they'd have seen, the places they'd have gone, the information they'd have acquired and the people they'd have met.

It make take some time for all of the images on this page to be transferred to your computer – please be patient!

Welcome to the TRAIN 2004 Conference! Dan and Judy stand ready to take walk-in registrations. Would you like to change your seminar choices after having pre-registered? As long as C-13 isn't on your list, it's probably not going to be problem.

Marlene helps out with the PowerPoint slides during the worship sessions with Doug Horley.

There were 20 exhibitors at the conference. Some were promoting their ministries; others had specialized products and materials available. Did one of them have something you could take back with you?

An attendee examines a book in the Resource Center to see if it's suitable for his ministry. Each attendee was given a coupon with their registration package just for this occasion.

Coffee, snacks and other refreshments were made available during the breaks between the seminars and plenary sessions.

One of the new tracks for 2004 was "Young Adult Ministry – Launching the Next-Gen". This series of seminars introduced us to a unique "next-gen" church plant, the concepts involved in leading it and the lessons we can all learn from it.

A group of attendees pay rapt attention in one of the 76 75 seminars offered this year. What they learn here will impact their ministries back in their own churches and organizations.

Jim has all of his props together as he's about to give his "Xtreme Ministry" seminar for youth ministry leaders – a media presentation with vision development and practical suggestions for using the outdoors in the context of ministry.

Les presents the "Gospel Ballooning" seminar – not a balloon art workshop, but the application of ballon art in the teaching of Biblical principles.

Sheryl takes a break from overseeing the TRAIN 2004 Conference's various activities. Now that the conference is over, we trust and pray that you found encouragement in the Lord, and that you will triumph over your challenges as you serve Him and His people.


See you in 2005!
